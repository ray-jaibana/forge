## When to Use
Before writing any blog post.

## Structure

### Title Formula
`[Target Keyword]: [Specific Benefit or Outcome]`
- Good: "Construction Project Budget Tracking: How to Stop Cost Overruns Before They Start"
- Bad: "Why Budgets Matter in Construction"

### Introduction (150-200 words)
1. Open with the pain point — in the reader's own words
2. State what this post will solve
3. No filler, no "In today's world..."

### Body (800-1,200 words)
- H2 headers with secondary keywords
- Practical steps (numbered lists beat walls of text)
- Real examples > generic advice
- Data points where possible (even rough stats)
- At least one screenshot or diagram

### Conclusion (100-150 words)
- Summarize the key takeaway in one sentence
- One clear CTA: "Start your free trial" / "See how SiteTrakr handles this"

## SEO Checklist
- [ ] Target keyword in title (first 60 chars)
- [ ] Target keyword in first paragraph
- [ ] Target keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Meta description: 150-160 chars, includes keyword, ends with action
- [ ] Internal links to 2-3 related posts
- [ ] Alt text on all images
- [ ] Word count: 1,200-1,800 (sweet spot for most topics)

## Tone
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague, not a textbook
- Active voice, short sentences
- No jargon without explanation
- Contractions are fine ("you're", "it's")

## Pitfalls
- Don't write about us — write about the reader's problem
- Don't keyword-stuff — use the term naturally
- Don't bury the value in the last paragraph — lead with it
